SUMMARY -- ALL YEARS (totals and frequencies for all 7 years so far!)

Orange County's bird populations appear to be in good shape.   Notice that grassland birds like Eastern Meadowlarks and Grasshopper Sparrows held their own in rural parts of the county.   Forest birds like the tanagers, Acadian Flycatchers, and Hooded Warblers were also well represented this year.

Crows and jays, the species apparently most susceptible to West Nile Virus, were present in about average numbers this year.

On the other hand, for some reason, Carolina Chickadees and Tufted Titmice fell to a 7-year low!
